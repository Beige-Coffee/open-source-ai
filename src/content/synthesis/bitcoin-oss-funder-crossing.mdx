This essay names the audience the grants section of this site is
built for: someone who used to fund Bitcoin OSS and is now considering
open-source AI. It also names the operational map of who is doing
similar work in the AI space, so the cross-over can be informed.

## What a Bitcoin OSS funder usually optimizes for

The Bitcoin OSS funding world (HRF, OpenSats, Spiral, Brink, Chaincode
Labs, the Bitcoin Open Source Project Fund, John Pfeffer's Atomic
Capital) shares a posture even when individual programs differ:

- **Independence from corporate gatekeepers.** Funded work should not
  depend on any single company's platform being friendly.
- **Permissionless infrastructure.** No KYC for users, no API keys
  required, no terms of service that can be changed unilaterally.
- **Long time horizons.** Software that has to outlast its funders
  and its current contributors.
- **Cypherpunk values.** Privacy by default, censorship resistance,
  user-controlled keys, individual sovereignty over state sovereignty.

Almost none of those values are explicitly on the cover of a typical
AI safety grant. Most AI grants optimize for capability research,
benchmark performance, or governance debate.

But there is a specific set of AI funders whose programs map cleanly
onto the Bitcoin-OSS posture. They are the natural targets for the
Bitcoin-OSS funder considering AI.

## The map (cypherpunk-adjacent AI funders)

**(Funder: hrf)** is the most direct crossover. HRF already funds
Bitcoin and Lightning work for human rights defenders. In November
2025 they announced an AI for Individual Rights program with eight
inaugural grantees. The thesis is the same as the Bitcoin work: tools
that put capability (in this case, AI inference and agency) into the
hands of dissidents and civil society under authoritarian regimes.
Notable grantees: (Grant: Maple AI), (Grant: Routstr), (Grant: OpenCode),
(Grant: PayPerQ), (Grant: CANVAS Global Education for Nonviolent Engagement (GENE)).

**(Funder: cosmos-institute)** funds philosopher-builders making
prototypes at the intersection of AI and human flourishing. Brendan
McCord's program. Emphasis on reason, decentralization, and individual
autonomy. Different from the AI safety mainstream because the
animating question is "what serves human flourishing" rather than
"what reduces existential risk." The (Funder: cosmos-fire) sub-program
specifically funds truth-seeking AI tools.

**(Funder: foresight-institute)** has the longest pedigree. Founded by
the Drexler / nanotech world; now funds quarterly AI safety,
cryptography, and longevity micro-grants. Cypherpunk-adjacent by
history. ~$5M / year distributed.

**(Funder: opensats)** funds Bitcoin, Lightning, and Nostr OSS.
Crossing into AI: (Grant: Orchard) integrates Ollama with Lightning
and Cashu. The general posture is that OpenSats funds AI work when
it is built on top of sovereign protocols, not as a separate program.

**(Funder: block-goose)** is Block's grant program for Goose, the
sovereignty-positioned open agent. Spiral (also Block) funds the
Bitcoin / Lightning OSS side. Block as a whole has both flanks active.

## The map (mainstream funders worth knowing)

The Bitcoin-OSS funder reading this should also know the larger
flows, even if the posture is different.

(Funder: open-philanthropy) is the largest discretionary AI safety
funder. Different posture (existential risk lens, not cypherpunk
lens). Worth tracking because it sets the funding climate for many
adjacent programs.

(Funder: future-of-life-institute) funds AI safety and multistakeholder
governance. Periodic themed RFPs.

(Funder: survival-and-flourishing-fund) is Jaan Tallinn's vehicle.
Distributes ~$30M / year to AI safety and longtermist work.
Recommendations are public. The S-Process methodology is interesting
even if the funded work is largely outside the cypherpunk frame.

(Funder: manifund) operates a regranting program for AI safety. Fast
grants, low friction. (Funder: lightspeed-grants) is in the same
neighborhood.

(Funder: nlnet) is the European public-funded counterpart. NGI Zero
Commons Fund, bi-monthly call. Skews protocols, identity, trusted
hardware. Real overlap with the cypherpunk posture but with EU public
funding conventions.

(Funder: sovereign-tech-fund) is the German government's program for
critical open-source infrastructure. Cross-cuts into AI runtime via
the underlying tools (curl, OpenSSH, GNU coreutils) that AI servers
depend on.

## How to use this map

If your existing thesis is "fund Bitcoin OSS that puts permissionless
money rails into hands that need them," your AI analog is:

- Tools that run inference locally on hardware the user owns
  (Project: ollama, Project: llama-cpp). The (Layer: runtime) page
  lists the production-grade open engines.
- Decentralized inference and training (Project: prime-intellect,
  Project: petals, Project: templar). The (Layer:
  sovereignty-decentralization) page covers this.
- Agent infrastructure that lets the user keep their data and choose
  their model (Project: goose, Project: openhands, Project: aider).
  The (Layer: agents) page covers this.
- Identity and verifiable computing (Project: ezkl, Project: phala,
  W3C DIDs). The (Layer: identity-trust) page covers this.
- Agentic payment rails: (Reading: L402: HTTP 402 Payment Required, For Real This Time)
  is the Lightning-anchored take, (Reading: x402: Stablecoin Payments for the AI Era)
  is the Coinbase alternative.

The chain through all of this is the three load-bearing claims:
(Essay: closed-silicon-defeats-open-weights), (Essay:
closed-runtime-defeats-open-silicon), (Essay:
closed-agents-defeat-open-weights). Funding any single layer in
isolation does not produce a sovereign stack. Funding the chain
does.

## What is not on this map

Funders that exclusively fund existential-risk research without an
OSS deliverable. Funders that exclusively fund AI policy work.
Funders that fund AI applications in domains (drug discovery, climate)
without an open-source-AI angle. They are real and they fund
important work; they are just not the natural target for a Bitcoin-OSS-
funder pivot. The (Layer: governance) page documents the broader
governance landscape.
