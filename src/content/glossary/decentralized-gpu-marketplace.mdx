A market where any provider with spare <G term="gpu">GPU</G> capacity can list it,
tenants bid or accept fixed prices, and settlement happens on a
shared protocol layer (a blockchain or a federated ledger). Akash
uses a reverse-auction on Cosmos with the AKT token; io.net
aggregates pre-existing GPU clusters into a single API on Solana;
Bittensor compute subnets (Subnet 27 and others) price compute via
TAO emissions tied to provider reputation.

The architectural bet is that aggregated independent capacity can
match the unit economics of hyperscaler clouds. Through 2025 to 2026
these markets have demonstrated they can serve <G term="inference">inference</G> and small-
scale <G term="fine-tuning">fine-tuning</G> competitively. The harder gap is <G term="frontier">frontier</G> training:
distributed training across loosely-connected nodes is research-grade
(Prime Intellect, Templar, Pluralis); commercial production training
on decentralized marketplaces remains rare.

For the sovereignty argument these markets are the most direct
counterweight to hyperscaler concentration. Whether they reach the
scale where they constrain hyperscaler pricing or remain niche
alternatives is one of the questions that will define the compute layer through 2026.
