The canonical decentralized-<G term="inference">inference</G> reference project. Petals
splits a <G term="transformer">transformer</G> into stages, runs each stage on a different
volunteer <G term="gpu">GPU</G> connected over the public internet, and forwards
activations between them. A user connects to the network as a client,
which routes the forward pass through a sequence of nodes serving
each stage.

The <G term="throughput">throughput</G> is dramatically lower than centralized serving (limits
imposed by inter-node <G term="latency">latency</G> over consumer internet), but the
architecture demonstrates that <G term="frontier">frontier</G>-scale models can run without
a single operator controlling the <G term="inference">inference</G>. <G term="fine-tuning">Fine-tuning</G> over the
swarm is also supported.

Petals is less a production-ready alternative to <G term="vllm">vLLM</G> and more a
proof-of-concept for sovereignty-aligned inference architecture. The
ideas (pipeline-parallel inference across loosely-connected nodes)
inform a broader research stream of decentralized AI compute.
