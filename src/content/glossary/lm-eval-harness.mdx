EleutherAI's evaluation harness. It implements a couple hundred
standardized <G term="benchmark">benchmark</G> tasks (<G term="mmlu">MMLU</G>, ARC, HellaSwag, GSM8K, IFEval,
GPQA, MUSR, BBH, and many more) with a unified runner that supports
<G term="hugging-face">Hugging Face</G> models, OpenAI-compatible APIs, <G term="vllm">vLLM</G>-served models, and
several other backends.

The harness is the reference implementation behind the Open LLM
<G term="leaderboard">Leaderboard</G> and behind most "we ran <G term="mmlu">MMLU</G> on our model and got 78.4"
claims in the open-weights community. Running standardized harness
results is the table-stakes evaluation step for any model release.

Two things to know. Version pinning matters: harness updates have
changed scoring conventions in ways that shift reported numbers by
several points. And the harness measures what it measures: an aggregate
of academic benchmarks. Agent and real-task evaluation needs separate
tooling (SWE-Bench harness, τ-bench harness, etc).
