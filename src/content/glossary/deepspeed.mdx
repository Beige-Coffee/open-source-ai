Microsoft's training optimization library. DeepSpeed introduced `ZeRO`,
the <G term="sharding">sharding</G> technique that lets data-parallel training cross the
memory boundary of any single <G term="gpu">GPU</G> by splitting optimizer states,
gradients, and parameters across the data-parallel group.

The library combines easily with PyTorch and with <G term="megatron">Megatron</G>'s
parallelism abstractions; a typical production training stack runs
<G term="megatron">Megatron</G>'s tensor-parallel + pipeline-parallel layout combined with
DeepSpeed's <G term="sharding">ZeRO sharding</G>. DeepSpeed-<G term="inference">Inference</G> adds a separate
<G term="inference">inference</G> path with tensor-parallel kernels, though for production
inference <G term="vllm">vLLM</G> and <G term="sglang">SGLang</G> have largely displaced it.

Full coverage at [/projects/deepspeed](/projects/deepspeed).
