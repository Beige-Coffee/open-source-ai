A nuclear reactor sized under ~300 MW electrical, with most components
fabricated in a factory and shipped to site rather than poured on-
site. The smaller size and modular construction promise shorter
build times and lower capital intensity per project than
conventional gigawatt-class reactors, though no commercial SMR has
yet operated at scale in the US as of early 2026.

For AI infrastructure SMRs entered the conversation in 2024 when
Google contracted Kairos Power for ~500 MW of SMR capacity, Amazon
took a stake in X-energy, and several hyperscalers entered into
options or offtake agreements. The appeal: SMR build times of 5 to 8
years (claimed) versus 15+ years for new conventional reactors, and
the option to site multiple units adjacent to a single data center
campus.

The skeptical read is that no operator has yet delivered an SMR in
the US on the schedule originally announced; the technology may
prove commercially viable but the timing to relieve current
gigawatt-class power shortages is uncertain.
