A vector of typically 384 to 4096 floats produced by an embedding model
from a chunk of text. The model is trained so that texts with similar
meaning produce vectors with high cosine similarity. The embedding
becomes a queryable handle on the text: index a million chunks as
vectors, embed a query, retrieve the nearest neighbors.

The model that produces embeddings is separate from the generative
model that consumes the retrieved text. Open embedding models (BGE,
GTE, Nomic, mxbai) compete on the MTEB <G term="benchmark">benchmark</G>, which evaluates
retrieval, classification, clustering, and <G term="reranking">reranking</G> quality across
languages and domains.

Dimensionality is a knob. Smaller vectors mean cheaper storage and
faster nearest-neighbor search at some quality cost. The Matryoshka
training approach (2024) produces embeddings whose first N dimensions
are usable on their own, letting a single model serve multiple
size-quality tradeoffs.
