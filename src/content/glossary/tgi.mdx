<G term="hugging-face">Hugging Face</G>'s open <G term="inference">inference</G> server, released in 2022 as one of the
first production-grade serving stacks for <G term="open-weights">open-weight</G> LLMs. TGI shipped
Rust-based request handling, Python-based model code, and <G term="cuda">CUDA</G> kernels
for <G term="transformer">transformer</G> <G term="attention">attention</G> and <G term="continuous-batching">continuous batching</G>.

Through 2023 TGI was the credible alternative to NVIDIA's
<G term="tensorrt-llm">TensorRT-LLM</G>. The release of <G term="vllm">vLLM</G> and the rapid maturation of <G term="sglang">SGLang</G>
in 2024 narrowed the niche; by 2026 TGI is in maintenance mode within
the <G term="hugging-face">Hugging Face</G> ecosystem, used mostly for legacy deployments and on
Hugging Face's own <G term="inference">Inference</G> Endpoints product.
