Apple's open-source array framework, MIT-licensed, designed around
Apple Silicon's unified-memory architecture. Where most ML frameworks
shuttle data between CPU and <G term="gpu">GPU</G> memory, MLX treats them as a single
pool, which suits Mac Studio configurations with 128 GB or 192 GB of
unified memory.

The framework supports both <G term="inference">inference</G> and training; for end users the
practical use case is running <G term="open-weights">open-weight</G> models on a Mac. MLX-LM and
MLX-VLM packages cover the major model families with PyTorch-style
APIs. Community-reported throughput for a quantized Llama-3-70B on a
Mac Studio M2 Ultra sits in the high single digits to low teens of
tokens per second; smaller models in the 7B-13B range run at tens to
low hundreds.

Full coverage at [/projects/apple-mlx](/projects/apple-mlx).
