A system where two or more LLM agents communicate to solve a problem.
Patterns vary: a planner agent splits work and delegates to specialist
sub-agents; a debater pair argues toward a synthesis; a research lead
spawns parallel investigators and aggregates their findings.

The empirical case for multi-agent is contested. Anthropic's published
research system uses one lead agent that spawns parallel sub-agents and
reports gains over single-agent baselines on open-ended research tasks.
Cognition's June 2025 "Don't Build Multi-Agents" essay argues the
opposite: that fragmenting state across agents loses critical context
and that single-agent designs are the safer default for software tasks.

Frameworks that target this shape: <G term="autogen">AutoGen</G>, CrewAI, LangGraph, AG2,
MetaGPT. They differ on communication topology (group chat vs hub-spoke
vs DAG) and on how shared state is represented. Whether multi-agent is
genuinely useful or a wrapper around a more careful single-agent design
is a live question through 2026.
