The exponential of the average negative log-likelihood per token. A
perplexity of N is interpretable as "the model is on average as
uncertain as if it were choosing uniformly among N options at each
step." Lower means the model assigned higher probability to the actual
text, which usually correlates with better downstream behavior.

Perplexity is the diagnostic of choice during <G term="pretraining">pretraining</G> and during
<G term="quantization">quantization</G> or <G term="fine-tuning">fine-tuning</G> experiments. A drop in perplexity on a
held-out set tells you the model improved at the next-token-prediction
task; a spike during <G term="quantization">quantization</G> tells you the precision loss is
biting.

For end-task quality perplexity is unreliable: a model can have lower
perplexity but lose at instruction following, refusal, or reasoning.
For training diagnostics it remains load-bearing because it is cheap
to compute and stable across runs in a way that aggregate benchmarks
are not.
