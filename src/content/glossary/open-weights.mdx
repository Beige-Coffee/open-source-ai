The 2024 to 2026 term-of-art for a model released as a downloadable
weight file but without the training data, training code, or both.
Most <G term="llama">Llama</G>, <G term="mistral">Mistral</G>, <G term="qwen">Qwen</G>, <G term="deepseek">DeepSeek</G>, and <G term="gemma">Gemma</G> releases are open-
weight: the parameters are public, the rest is not. Under the <G term="osaid">OSAID</G>
definition this does not qualify as open-source AI.

The distinction matters in practice. With open weights you can run,
<G term="fine-tuning">fine-tune</G>, and inspect the model. You cannot reproduce the training
run, audit what the model was trained on, or replicate the safety and
quality choices made during <G term="post-training">post-training</G>. For most research and
deployment purposes this is acceptable; for legal compliance, audit,
or scientific reproducibility it often is not.

The label has come to function as a useful compromise term. Saying "open
weights" is honest about what was and was not released, where "open
source" tends to overclaim. The <G term="osi">OSI</G>'s position is that "open weights"
is fine as a description; it is the upgrade to "open source AI" that
requires more.
