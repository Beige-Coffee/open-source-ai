The parallelism strategy that <G term="mixture-of-experts">mixture-of-experts</G>
models use to spread their experts across multiple GPUs. Each GPU
holds a subset of the experts; on every forward pass, tokens are
routed to the experts that should process them, and the GPUs do an
all-to-all collective to exchange tokens with their assigned experts.

Expert parallelism's strength is that it makes large MoE models
serveable on a fleet. <G term="deepseek">DeepSeek</G> V3's 671B
total parameters across 256 experts can't fit on a single GPU; spread
across an 8xH100 node with expert parallelism, each GPU holds 32
experts (~85B parameters) and the all-to-all shuffles tokens to the
right experts per step. <G term="mixture-of-experts">Mixtral</G> 8x7B
similarly benefits.

The weakness is interconnect: all-to-all traffic is denser than
tensor parallelism's all-reduce because every GPU sends data to
every other GPU on every routing step. On nodes without
<G term="nvlink">NVLink</G> or NVSwitch, the all-to-all becomes the
bottleneck and expert parallelism can be slower than other
strategies. DeepSeek V3's published recipe explicitly trades off
expert parallelism, tensor parallelism, and pipeline parallelism
depending on the interconnect topology. Production serving engines
(vLLM, SGLang, TensorRT-LLM) implement expert parallelism as one
of several parallelism modes the operator picks at deployment.
