In an <G term="inference">inference</G> runtime: the loop that picks which requests to include
in the next batch, when to admit a new request, when to evict cache
blocks. <G term="vllm">vLLM</G> and <G term="sglang">SGLang</G> both use continuous-<G term="batching">batching</G> schedulers that
make these decisions at the token level. The scheduler's policy
determines the <G term="throughput">throughput</G>-vs-<G term="latency">latency</G> curve of the whole engine.

In a training cluster: the workload manager (Slurm, Kubernetes, Ray,
custom) that places jobs on physical hardware, handles preemption,
and matches resource requests to availability. Hyperscaler training
clusters use custom schedulers tuned for <G term="gpu">GPU</G>-aware placement,
gang-scheduling of distributed jobs, and fault-tolerance handoffs.

Scheduling design surfaces in user-visible ways. Cold-start <G term="latency">latency</G>
on a "scale to zero" serverless <G term="inference">inference</G> endpoint comes from the
scheduler waiting on hardware to provision. Long queue times on
shared clusters come from the scheduler's fairness policies. The
scheduler is rarely the thing people demo, but it decides who runs and who waits.
