A data-transfer mechanism where the network adapter writes directly
into the remote machine's memory through DMA, bypassing the kernel
and the receiving CPU. <G term="latency">Latency</G> drops by an order of magnitude
compared to TCP/IP; <G term="throughput">throughput</G> approaches the wire speed.

For AI training, RDMA is what makes cross-node collective operations
(all-reduce, all-gather) viable. The NCCL library that PyTorch,
<G term="deepspeed">DeepSpeed</G>, and <G term="megatron">Megatron</G> use to synchronize gradients depends on RDMA
under the hood. Without it, the network would dominate iteration time.

Two implementations matter in practice. <G term="infiniband">InfiniBand</G>-native RDMA is the
default on most large training clusters. RoCE (RDMA over Converged
Ethernet) runs the same semantics over standard Ethernet hardware,
trading some performance for lower cost and easier operations. Most
hyperscaler in-house clusters use RoCE; most research-lab clusters
use <G term="infiniband">InfiniBand</G>; the lines are blurring.
