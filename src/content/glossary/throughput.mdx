The aggregate rate of token output across all concurrent requests on a
given hardware unit. A <G term="vllm">vLLM</G> server might do 5000 tokens/sec on an
H100 at 70B <G term="llama">Llama</G> with <G term="fp8">FP8</G> weights; an MI300X might do 4000 tokens/sec
on the same model; an <G term="ollama">Ollama</G> instance on a Mac Studio does 50 tokens/
sec for a single user.

Throughput trades against <G term="latency">latency</G>. Larger batches push throughput up
at the cost of higher time-to-first-token and time-per-token for the
individual request. Production serving usually picks a target <G term="latency">latency</G>
budget and then tunes <G term="batching">batching</G>, <G term="quantization">quantization</G>, and <G term="prefix-caching">prefix caching</G> to
maximize throughput under that budget.

The relevant denominator depends on the question. Per-<G term="gpu">GPU</G> throughput
is the unit of hardware planning. Aggregate cluster throughput is the
unit of capacity planning. Tokens per dollar is the unit of business
planning. The same model can rank differently on each.
