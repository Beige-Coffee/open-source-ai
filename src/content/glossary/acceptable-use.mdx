A list of prohibited uses attached to many <G term="open-weights">open-weight</G> licenses. The
<G term="llama">Llama</G> AUP bars use for weapons, CSAM, harassment, and impersonation,
among others. <G term="gemma">Gemma</G>'s terms have a similar list. Most "ethical source"
licenses (Hippocratic, Anti-996, OpenRAIL) work the same way.

The clauses are non-controversial in spirit and controversial in form.
On the substance, almost no one defends the prohibited uses. On the
form, the <G term="osi">OSI</G> position is that any use restriction violates the Open
Source Definition's "no discrimination against fields of endeavor"
clause. The result: <G term="llama">Llama</G> and most ethical-source licenses are not
<G term="osaid">OSAID</G>-compliant even when the prohibited list is uncontroversial.

For users the practical implication is verification burden. A
licensee accepting an acceptable-use clause is taking on enforcement
risk if downstream use violates it; the licensor may revoke the
license. <G term="apache-2-0">Apache 2.0</G> weights carry no such revocation hook.
