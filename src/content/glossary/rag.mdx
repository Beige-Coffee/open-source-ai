The architectural pattern that bridges fixed-weight models and changing
data. A user query is embedded, the <G term="embedding">embedding</G> is matched against a
<G term="vector-database">vector database</G> of pre-embedded document chunks, the top-k chunks are
inserted into the prompt as context, and the model generates conditioned
on them. Citations naturally fall out as references to the retrieved
chunks.

RAG matters because it sidesteps the freshness problem (parametric
knowledge is frozen at the training cutoff) and the source-attribution
problem (a RAG answer can point at a specific paragraph). It is the
backbone of nearly every production chatbot on proprietary data.

Complications stack. <G term="chunking">Chunking</G> strategy matters: too small loses
context, too large blunts retrieval. <G term="reranking">Reranking</G> is usually needed
because vector similarity alone is noisy. Long-context models do not
obsolete RAG: the "lost in the middle" effect means even a 1M-token
window does not match precise retrieval into a smaller working context.
