The practice of running many examples through a model in parallel.
GPUs are compute-dense and memory-bandwidth-limited; running one
sequence at a time leaves most of the compute idle. Batching amortizes
the memory traffic across more useful work.

For training the batch size is mostly a quality knob (small batches
generalize differently, large batches require learning-rate tuning).
For <G term="inference">inference</G>, batching is the central knob: larger batches mean
higher <G term="throughput">throughput</G> per <G term="gpu">GPU</G> but higher per-request <G term="latency">latency</G>.

`Continuous batching` is the <G term="inference">inference</G>-time pattern that makes
batching dynamic per token instead of per request. Combined with
`PagedAttention` for cache management, modern runtimes maintain
batches of hundreds of concurrent requests per <G term="gpu">GPU</G> on production
workloads.
