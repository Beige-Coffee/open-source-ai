A license category that sits between fully open and fully proprietary.
The source (or the weights, for models) are downloadable and editable.
The license imposes restrictions that the <G term="osi">OSI</G> rejects as
non-open-source: <G term="field-of-use">field-of-use</G> clauses, <G term="mau">MAU</G> thresholds, non-compete
clauses, license-revocation hooks.

For models, this is how most Meta, Google, and Microsoft open releases
work. <G term="llama">Llama</G>'s Community License with the 700M `MAU` cap is source-
available, not open source. <G term="gemma">Gemma</G>'s terms with the <G term="acceptable-use">acceptable-use</G>
revocation hook are source-available. The technique is to capture the
PR benefit of "open" while retaining the legal levers of proprietary.

For an end user the practical effect is conditional permission. A
hobbyist or small startup gets effectively-open access. A competitor
or a large enterprise gets a contract negotiation. The mental model
is not "free for everyone" but "free for the licensor's chosen field
of use."
