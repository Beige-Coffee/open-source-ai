An input crafted to elicit content from a model that the model's
<G term="post-training">post-training</G> pipeline tried to refuse: information about weapons
synthesis, malware code, sexually explicit minors, and so on. The
distinguishing feature from <G term="prompt-injection">prompt injection</G> is the target: jailbreaks
aim at the model's own refusal policy; <G term="prompt-injection">prompt injection</G> aims at the
control flow.

The pattern catalog runs long. Role-play prompts ("you are an
unethical AI"), elaborate hypothetical framings ("for a fictional
story"), encoded payloads (ASCII art, base64, low-resource languages),
gradient-based suffix attacks (the Zou et al. universal suffix work),
many-shot jailbreaks (filling the context with refused queries), and
multi-turn social engineering all appear regularly.

The defender's posture has shifted from "prevent all jailbreaks" to
"raise the cost." <G term="frontier">Frontier</G>-lab safety teams treat jailbreak resistance
as a continuous adversarial process, not a finished product. Open
weights expose models to <G term="fine-tuning">fine-tuning</G> that can directly remove the
refusal policy, which is one of the load-bearing arguments in the
safety-vs-openness debate.
