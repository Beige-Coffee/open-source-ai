Google's in-house alternative to GPUs. The architecture centers on a
large systolic array of multiply-accumulate units that dataflow
optimally for matrix-matrix multiplication, with on-chip memory bigger
than <G term="gpu">GPU</G> caches and interconnect (ICI) designed for tightly-coupled
pods of thousands of chips.

TPUs are the substrate Google uses to train Gemini and serve consumer
AI products. They are available externally through Google Cloud (v5e,
v5p, Trillium, Ironwood generations). The software stack is JAX and
XLA rather than <G term="cuda">CUDA</G>, which is the main reason TPUs do not dominate
beyond Google: most open-source training and <G term="inference">inference</G> code targets
<G term="cuda">CUDA</G> first.

Worth knowing because the design choices in TPUs influence the broader
silicon discussion: systolic arrays as the answer to "we only need
matmul," tightly-coupled pods as the answer to "model parallelism is
the bottleneck," and software-defined network topology as the answer
to "we want to schedule trains and serves on the same fleet."
