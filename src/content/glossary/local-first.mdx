A design principle. <G term="inference">Inference</G> happens on the user's hardware (laptop,
phone, edge device). The user keeps custody of prompts, outputs, and
any retrieved data. Sync to the cloud, if any, is opt-in and
end-to-end encrypted. The reference point is the 2019 Ink & Switch
essay on local-first software, predating modern LLMs but mapping
cleanly onto them.

For LLMs the practical limit is model size. <G term="llama">Llama</G>-3-8B and <G term="mistral">Mistral</G>-7B
run well on consumer hardware via `llama.cpp` and `Ollama`; <G term="llama">Llama</G>-3-70B
fits on a high-end Mac or pair of consumer GPUs; <G term="frontier">frontier</G>-scale models
(400B+ parameters) do not. The trend through 2024 to 2026 of smaller-
but-capable models (Phi, <G term="qwen">Qwen</G>-7B, <G term="gemma">Gemma</G>-3) has expanded what local-
first can credibly serve.

The sovereignty case is direct. Local-first <G term="inference">inference</G> means no third
party sees the user's queries. The Apple Intelligence design (mostly
<G term="on-device">on-device</G>, with confidential-compute fallback) and the Maple AI
posture (local-first plus <G term="tee">TEE</G> fallback) are the two most prominent
2026 productizations of the pattern.
