A moving target rather than a fixed line. "Frontier" labels the
capability envelope set by the top models from the labs investing the
most in <G term="pretraining">pretraining</G>: OpenAI's GPT and o series, Anthropic's Claude
family, Google's Gemini, xAI's Grok at the top end. <G term="open-weights">Open-weight</G>
families approach but rarely lead the frontier; <G term="deepseek">DeepSeek</G>-V3 was the
clearest near-frontier open release through early 2025.

The term carries governance weight. The UK and EU regulatory regimes,
US executive orders, and the Frontier Model Forum's self-regulation
all draw a line at "frontier" compute thresholds (often defined as
training runs exceeding 10^25 or 10^26 floating-point operations) and
apply specific reporting, evaluation, or safety-case obligations there.

For open AI the implication is unsettling. The capability gap between
frontier closed models and frontier <G term="open-weights">open weights</G> has been roughly six
to twelve months through 2024 to 2026. Whether the gap closes,
widens, or holds depends on questions about open compute, open data,
and whether frontier labs continue to release weights at all.
