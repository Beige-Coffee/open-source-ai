Google's <G term="open-weights">open-weight</G> line, launched February 2024 as a 2B/7B model
pair, since extended to 9B and 27B base sizes (Gemma 2) and to
multimodal and code-specific variants. The architecture and <G term="tokenizer">tokenizer</G>
inherit from Gemini research; the goal of the release is to give
researchers and developers access to Google's <G term="open-weights">open-weight</G> contribution.

The license terms are notable. Gemma is <G term="source-available">source-available</G>, not open
source: an <G term="acceptable-use">acceptable-use</G> clause and a license-revocation hook
distinguish it from Apache-licensed weights. The use restrictions
parallel <G term="llama">Llama</G>'s; the revocation hook is more aggressive.
