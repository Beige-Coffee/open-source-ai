The model files. Having the weights means you can run the model,
fine-tune it, and inspect it; it does not mean you can re-train or
reproduce it. This is the most superficial layer of openness and also
where license diversity hides the most: Apache 2.0 is genuinely open;
the Llama Community License and Gemma's terms are source-available with
revocable acceptable-use clauses; OLMo is one of the few families open
at the weights, data, and training-code layers all at once. The
open-weights ecosystem in 2026 is dominated by Chinese labs (Qwen,
DeepSeek, Kimi, GLM), Meta's Llama family, Mistral, Google's Gemma, and
AI2's OLMo. The OSAID definition decides which of these counts as "open
source AI"; under the strictest reading, most do not.
