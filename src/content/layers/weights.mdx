The model files. Having the weights means you can run the model,
<G term="fine-tuning">fine-tune</G> it, and inspect it; it does not mean you can re-train or
reproduce it. This is the most superficial layer of openness and also
where license diversity hides the most: <G term="apache-2-0">Apache 2.0</G> is genuinely open;
the <G term="llama">Llama</G> Community License and <G term="gemma">Gemma</G>'s terms are <G term="source-available">source-available</G> with
revocable <G term="acceptable-use">acceptable-use</G> clauses; OLMo is one of the few families open
at the weights, data, and training-code layers all at once. The
open-weights ecosystem in 2026 is dominated by Chinese labs (<G term="qwen">Qwen</G>,
<G term="deepseek">DeepSeek</G>, Kimi, GLM), Meta's <G term="llama">Llama family</G>, <G term="mistral">Mistral</G>, Google's <G term="gemma">Gemma</G>, and
AI2's OLMo. The <G term="osaid">OSAID</G> definition decides which of these counts as "open
source AI"; under the strictest reading, most do not.
