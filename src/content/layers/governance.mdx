Definitions, licenses, foundations, and standards bodies. Governance
does not produce silicon, weights, or runtime, but it produces the
definitions by which the other layers get judged. The dominant body is
the <G term="osi">Open Source Initiative</G>; <G term="osaid">OSAID</G> v1.0 (October 2024) is the first
formal definition of "open source AI" and is contested mostly around
whether full training-data disclosure should be required. Other actors:
the Linux Foundation hosts AAIF and <G term="a2a">A2A</G>, the Apache Foundation stewards
<G term="apache-2-0">Apache 2.0</G>, and the lab-specific license families (<G term="llama">Llama</G> Community
License, <G term="gemma">Gemma</G> Terms, <G term="mistral">Mistral</G> Research Tiers) operate outside the <G term="osi">OSI</G>
process and accumulate their own ecosystems by default. The 2026 <G term="osaid">OSAID</G>
revision cycle is the operational moment for definitional rigor.
