The cryptographic and hardware infrastructure that lets you verify what
a model is doing or who an agent is. Four sub-clusters share this
layer: Trusted Execution Environments (<G term="sgx">Intel SGX</G>, AMD SEV, NVIDIA H100
<G term="confidential-computing">Confidential Computing</G>, Apple Secure Enclave); confidential-computing
services (Apple PCC, Phala, AWS Nitro Enclaves); <G term="verifiable-inference">verifiable inference</G>
(EZKL, Modulus, Lagrange's <G term="zkml">ZKML</G>); and agent-identity primitives (DIDs,
verifiable credentials, AAIF agent passports, <G term="l402">L402</G> macaroons). Trust
primitives depend on silicon vendor cooperation, which means the
silicon-vendor monopoly at Layer 1 can re-entrench at the trust layer
unless open-silicon counter-bets develop their own trust-anchor stories.
<G term="zkml">ZKML</G> tries to make trust silicon-agnostic via pure cryptography, at
substantial performance cost.
