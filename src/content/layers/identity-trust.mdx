The cryptographic and hardware infrastructure that lets you verify what
a model is doing or who an agent is. Four sub-clusters share this
layer: Trusted Execution Environments (Intel SGX, AMD SEV, NVIDIA H100
Confidential Computing, Apple Secure Enclave); confidential-computing
services (Apple PCC, Phala, AWS Nitro Enclaves); verifiable inference
(EZKL, Modulus, Lagrange's ZKML); and agent-identity primitives (DIDs,
verifiable credentials, AAIF agent passports, L402 macaroons). Trust
primitives depend on silicon vendor cooperation, which means the
silicon-vendor monopoly at Layer 1 can re-entrench at the trust layer
unless open-silicon counter-bets develop their own trust-anchor stories.
ZKML tries to make trust silicon-agnostic via pure cryptography, at
substantial performance cost.
