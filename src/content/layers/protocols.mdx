## What this layer is

The wire protocols agents, tools, and models use to talk to each other.
This is the layer of integration, and it is where the most consequential
standards decisions for the AI ecosystem are being made in 2025-2026.
The big three: MCP (Model Context Protocol, Anthropic-seeded), ACP
(Agent Client Protocol), and A2A (Agent-to-Agent, Google-seeded). Plus
the agentic payments protocols: L402, x402, MCP commerce extensions.

Protocols matter for sovereignty disproportionately to their visibility.
Whoever owns the protocol owns the integration ecosystem. HTTP, SMTP,
BGP, TCP are the analogous historical examples; their openness allowed
the internet to be a platform anyone could build on. If MCP becomes the
analogous protocol for AI tools, and MCP remains genuinely open, that
is a sovereignty win. If MCP ends up under one vendor's de-facto control
(Anthropic's seeded status, the spec's evolution path), the dynamic
shifts.

## Open vs. closed

The protocols here are notionally open (specs are public, implementations
are permissive-licensed), but each carries different governance shapes
that affect how open they remain over time.

**MCP** (Model Context Protocol) is Anthropic's protocol for connecting
models to context sources and tools. The spec is at modelcontextprotocol.io;
implementations exist in TypeScript, Python, Go, Rust. MCP has become
the de facto standard for tool access in agents in 2025-2026: supported
by Claude Code natively, by Goose, by OpenHands, by every serious open
agent. Donated to the Linux Foundation's AAIF in December 2025.

**A2A** (Agent-to-Agent) is Google's protocol for inter-agent
communication, announced April 2025 with broad industry signatories.
A2A complements MCP: MCP is for an agent to talk to tools and resources,
A2A is for agents to talk to other agents. Now under the Linux
Foundation as of 2025.

**AAIF** (Agentic AI Foundation) is the Linux Foundation umbrella for
agent-and-protocol governance. Founded December 2025 with platinum
members AWS, Anthropic, Block, Bloomberg, Cloudflare, Google, Microsoft,
OpenAI.

The agentic payments rails are a separate but related cluster. **L402**
(Lightning Labs's payment protocol pairing HTTP 402 with Lightning
Network invoices) is the original; **x402** (Coinbase's 2025 revival of
HTTP 402 with on-chain payments) is the alternative. **MCP commerce** is
the early-stage proposal to extend MCP itself with payment primitives.

## The lock-in vector

Protocols are a governance lock-in vector. Once a protocol is in the
field, it is hard to replace; once one vendor controls the evolution,
it is hard to fork. The summit-grade framing: open protocol specs are
necessary but not sufficient; governance shapes that distribute
authority over evolution (multi-vendor consortium, IETF-style RFC
process, AAIF-style neutral foundation) are what makes a protocol
durably open.

The agentic-payments protocols are a sub-argument: if AI agents become
economic actors, the payment rails determine who captures the value.
L402 and x402 are the two open-spec offerings; closed equivalents are
emerging (Stripe Agent SDK is the cleanest example). Whichever protocol
wins the agentic-payments standard battle locks in the rails for a
decade.
