Where the math has to physically happen. Data centers, power
generation, cooling architecture, and the grid interconnect that
determine whether a multi-gigawatt training run can even start.
Through 2025 and 2026 the binding constraint at the <G term="frontier">frontier</G> moved
from chip allocation to electricity: Microsoft restarted Three Mile
Island to feed Azure AI ([Constellation press release, September 2024](https://www.constellationenergy.com/newsroom/2024/Constellation-to-Launch-Crane-Clean-Energy-Center-Restoring-Jobs-and-Carbon-Free-Power-to-The-Grid.html)),
OpenAI / SoftBank / Oracle launched a $500B Stargate buildout
([OpenAI Stargate announcement, January 2025](https://openai.com/index/announcing-the-stargate-project/)),
and several sovereign-state programs (UAE G42, Saudi Humain, IndiaAI
Mission) entered the capex race. This is the layer where almost no
artifact is open-source in the <G term="osi">OSI</G> sense; the editorial focus is
concentration versus distribution: whether 5 GW of compute lives in
4 hyperscaler regions or 40 operators across 20 jurisdictions is the
load-bearing question for whether sovereign / individual AI is
technically possible. Decentralized compute markets (Akash, io.net,
Bittensor compute subnets, Crusoe's waste-flare-gas siting) and
<G term="behind-the-meter">behind-the-meter</G> / sovereign initiatives are the counterweights this
site tracks against the hyperscaler default.
