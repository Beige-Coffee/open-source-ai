The training corpora. The text, code, images, audio, and synthetic data
that go into pretraining and fine-tuning. This is the layer where "open"
is most ambiguous: almost every open-weights model keeps its training
data secret or only partially discloses it. Genuinely open data exists
for a small set of models (OLMo and Dolma, RedPajama, FineWeb, Common
Pile), built mostly as filtered subsets of Common Crawl. The legal and
licensing landscape is the binding constraint: licensed-data deals are
non-transferable, NYT v. OpenAI is unresolved, and the OSAID debate
hinges on whether disclosure of data is required for a release to be
"open source" at all.
