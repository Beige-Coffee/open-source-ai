The training corpora. The text, code, images, audio, and synthetic data
that go into <G term="pretraining">pretraining</G> and <G term="fine-tuning">fine-tuning</G>. This is the layer where "open"
is most ambiguous: almost every open-weights model keeps its training
data secret or only partially discloses it. Genuinely open data exists
for a small set of models (OLMo and Dolma, <G term="redpajama">RedPajama</G>, <G term="fineweb">FineWeb</G>, Common
<G term="the-pile">Pile</G>), built mostly as filtered subsets of <G term="common-crawl">Common Crawl</G>. The legal and
licensing landscape is the binding constraint: licensed-data deals are
non-transferable, NYT v. OpenAI is unresolved, and the <G term="osaid">OSAID</G> debate
hinges on whether disclosure of data is required for a release to be
"open source" at all.
