## What this layer is

The political and architectural patterns that distinguish "open AI"
from "open AI you can actually own." Sovereignty is a meta-layer
because sovereignty arguments apply at every production-pipeline
layer: open silicon, decentralized compute, open data, decentralized
training, self-hostable runtime, personal-memory agents, permissionless
payment rails.

This meta-layer also names the **framing distinction** that the rest of
the site is built on: individual sovereignty (cypherpunk, Bitcoin-and-Lightning-adjacent,
personal ownership of compute and data) versus state sovereignty (the
NVIDIA / Mistral / G42 "Sovereign AI" framing where the actor is a
nation-state). The two share the word and almost nothing else; one
framing accepts closed-but-domestic AI as sovereign, the other does not.

## What is in it

**Decentralized training** projects: Prime Intellect's INTELLECT-1 / 2 / 3,
Nous Research's DisTrO, Templar's Covenant-72B (Bittensor subnet),
Gensyn, Pluralis. Sub-cluster of the Training layer (Layer 4) that
specifically aims at sovereignty-positive distributed training over
commodity internet.

**Decentralized inference**: Petals-class projects that pipeline
inference for large models across many small machines. As of 2026 this
is viable for small models per provider and for cooperative-cluster
medium-model inference; not yet viable for frontier-scale inference
over the open internet.

**Local-first patterns**: llama.cpp on Apple Silicon, Ollama on
consumer hardware, MLX on Mac clusters, the "personal AI server"
category (an internal team Aegis, Friend, various Tab devices). Hardware-anchored
sovereignty.

**Permissionless agentic payments**: L402 (Lightning Labs), x402
(Coinbase), MCP commerce. Discussed in detail at the Protocols layer
but listed here as a sovereignty primitive because the choice of
payment rail determines who can become an economic actor in the
agentic web without permission.

**Open-source funding ecosystems**: NLnet, Mozilla.ai, Linux Foundation,
Optimism Retro Funding, Gitcoin Grants, NSF / Schmidt Sciences / Open
Philanthropy. The grants section of this site is the operational view
into this sub-layer.

## The lock-in vector

Sovereignty primitives have an interesting inverse-lock-in story. Each
primitive (decentralized training, local-first inference, permissionless
payments, open hardware) by itself does not change the production
economics enough to displace the centralized incumbent. Combined, the
primitives compound into a complete alternative stack that an
individual or a small organization can run end-to-end on hardware they
own.

The risk is that any single layer slipping closed defeats the
combination. The Sovereign AI Summit's core thesis is that closed
silicon defeats open weights, closed runtime defeats open silicon,
closed agents defeat open weights. This meta-layer is where those
arguments cross-reference each other.
