How weights become <G term="inference">inference</G>. The engines that take a <G term="transformer">transformer</G> model
and serve tokens efficiently on actual hardware. Runtime is the layer
most invisible to end users but most decisive for production economics:
the same model can cost ten times more on a poorly-tuned runtime than a
well-tuned one. This is also the layer where the open ecosystem has done
its most surprising work. <G term="vllm">vLLM</G> (<G term="paged-attention">PagedAttention</G>, production-grade),
<G term="sglang">SGLang</G> (<G term="radix-attention">RadixAttention</G>, structured generation), and <G term="llama-cpp">llama.cpp</G>
(<G term="local-first">local-first</G>, <G term="gguf">GGUF</G>) are open projects that repeatedly catch up to or
beat closed equivalents like <G term="tensorrt-llm">TensorRT-LLM</G>, often within months. When the
workload is well-defined, open runtime wins on technical merits even
when closed runtime still dominates hyperscaler revenue.
