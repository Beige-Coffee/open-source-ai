## Fixes applied (a-f)

- a2a: "By late 2025 the Linux Foundation took stewardship and the spec converged with parts of MCP for the tool-invocation layer" was wrong on both date and convergence. Replaced with verified facts: Google donated A2A to the Linux Foundation in June 2025 at OSSNA; founding partners AWS, Cisco, Microsoft, Salesforce, SAP, ServiceNow; A2A and MCP coexist (agents use MCP for tools, A2A for inter-agent coordination), no actual spec convergence has been documented.
- agentic-payments: x402 was claimed to use "stablecoin or Lightning settlement" but x402 actually uses USDC stablecoin only (Lightning is L402's domain). Also pinned dates: x402 (Coinbase, May 2025), L402 (Lightning Labs, March 2020), AP2 (Google, September 2025).
- alibi: Original claim "models trained at 1024 tokens generalized to 4096+ tokens" was wrong. The Press et al. 2022 paper only demonstrates extrapolation from 1024 to 2048 tokens; the paper does not report 4096+ results. Replaced with the verified 1024 → 2048 claim.
- apache-2-0: Original list of "meaningful Apache-2.0 open-weight families" included Phi-3 (which is actually MIT, not Apache 2.0) and Falcon (which is TII Falcon License derived from Apache 2.0 with an acceptable-use addendum, not pure Apache 2.0). Rewrote that paragraph to note Phi ships under MIT and Falcon under the TII Falcon License.
- common-crawl: Summary said "maintained since 2008" but Common Crawl was actually founded in 2007 and its corpus runs since 2008 crawls; commoncrawl.org now says "free, open corpus since 2007". Changed 2008 to 2007.
- confidential-computing: Original prose said "the AWS Nitro enclaves used by Apple"; Apple Private Cloud Compute does NOT use AWS Nitro Enclaves — it uses Apple's own custom Apple-silicon servers with Secure Enclave/Secure Boot. Rewrote to list these as separate examples.
- field-of-use: "several Microsoft DeepGEM releases" appeared to be fabricated (DeepGEMM is a DeepSeek FP8 kernel library, not a Microsoft model). Replaced with verifiable example: early LLaMA 1 weights from Meta (which were research-only at launch) plus Microsoft Research model previews.
